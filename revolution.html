A Century of Uncertainty

Quantum physics is the foundational theory that describes the nature and behavior of matter and energy at the atomic and subatomic level. Its emergence at the turn of the 20th century shattered the deterministic worldview of classical (Newtonian) physics. The quantum revolution began with the realization that energy is not continuous but comes in discrete packets, or "quanta." This discovery led to the development of radical concepts like wave-particle duality, superposition, and entanglement, which continue to drive modern technology and research.

THE FOUNDING FATHERS

WHO: Max Planck

**The Inventor (1900):** Planck is considered the father of quantum theory. He introduced the idea that energy is radiated and absorbed in **discrete units (quanta)** to explain black-body radiation, marking the birth of quantum mechanics.

HOW: Albert Einstein

**The Developer (1905):** Einstein solidified the quantum concept by using Planck's quanta to explain the photoelectric effect. He proposed that light itself consists of particles (**photons**), confirming that energy is fundamentally quantized.

HOW DEVELOPED: Niels Bohr

**The Builder (1913):** Bohr used quantization to develop his model of the atom, proposing that electrons only orbit in **specific, discrete shells**, which explained why atoms emit light at only specific wavelengths.

QUANTUM HISTORY QUIZ

Q1: What year is generally considered the birth year of quantum physics?

**Answer:** **1900**. This is the year Max Planck published his findings on black-body radiation, introducing the concept of the quantum.

Q2: Which major phenomenon did Einstein explain using the concept of quanta?

**Answer:** The **Photoelectric Effect**. This demonstrated that light behaves as discrete particles (photons), not just waves.

Q3: What classical physics concept did the discovery of quanta replace?

**Answer:** The belief that **energy is continuous**. Classical physics assumed energy could be emitted and absorbed in any amount, whereas quantum physics proved it comes in discrete packets.
